Step 1: Choose Your Ancestry

Your ancestry is your character's fundamental biological and cultural heritage. It determines your innate capabilities, grants you unique gifts, and positions you within the complex social tapestry of the post-Silence galaxy.

Human

The adaptable survivors, scattered and diversified across the galaxy.

Ancestry Gifts:

Adaptive Spirit: Once per session, when you fail an action roll, spend 2 Hope to gain advantage on your next roll of the same type.

Cultural Bridge: During a rest, mark a Stress to ask the GM one question about a group's motivations or customs.

Zephyrian

Stardust dancers, psychically sensitive and empathic beings.

Ancestry Gifts:

Veil Sensitivity: You automatically sense strong Veil phenomena and emotional states within Very Close range.

Emotional Resonance: Once per scene, you may convert 1 Stress from yourself or an ally into 1 Hope.

Kryllian

The unbreakable forge, four-armed natural engineers.

Ancestry Gifts:

Master Craftsman: When making rolls related to crafting or repairing, mark a Stress to gain advantage.

Structural Intuition: Once per session, mark a Stress to ask the GM about a system's most significant weakness.

Ethereal

Ghosts in the machine, energy beings with temporal perception.

Ancestry Gifts:

Phase Shift: Once per scene, mark a Stress to become incorporeal until the end of your next turn, allowing you to pass through solid objects and become immune to physical attacks.

Temporal Perception: You can sense temporal anomalies and distortions within Close range. Once per session, you can ask the GM about the most likely immediate consequence of a proposed action.

Synthetic

Liberated minds, self-aware AIs seeking purpose.

Ancestry Gifts:

Modular Consciousness: Once per long rest, you can temporarily download a new skill or knowledge set. Choose a trait; you gain advantage on rolls using that trait until your next long rest.

Digital Interface: You can directly interface with electronic systems and networks. You have advantage on rolls to hack, operate, or understand technological devices.

Rift Spinner

Time weavers, arachnids who manipulate causality.

Ancestry Gifts:

Chrono-Gift: Once per session, after any roll within Close range, spend 2 Hope to force a reroll.

Wall Weaver: You can move on vertical or inverted surfaces as normal ground. While touching a surface, you sense vibrations through it, gaining +2 on Perception checks to detect moving creatures within Close range that are also in contact with it.

Celestari

Descendants of stars, radiant beings with cosmic grace.

Ancestry Gifts:

Stellar Meditation: Once per long rest, meditate for 10 minutes to mark a Stress and gain 2 Hope.

Celestial Grace: Your unarmed attacks deal d6 magic damage. Once per session, heal the same damage dealt to nearby allies.

Kryx

Voices of the hunt, powerful and honor-based pack culture.

Ancestry Gifts:

Pack Intuition: When an ally within Very Close range is targeted by an attack, mark a Stress to gain advantage on your next attack against that foe.

Biolume Howl: Your unarmed attacks deal d6 magic damage. Once per session, mark a Stress to make your target Dizzied and gain 1 Hope.

Step 2: Define Your Community

If ancestry is your character's seed, community is the soil in which it germinated. It's the environment that shaped you during your formative years, the crucible where your beliefs, prejudices, and fundamental skills were forged.

Core World

Glittering Spires and Social Labyrinths

Vast data-nets, towering arcologies, and complex social hierarchies define life here. You know how to work the system.

Feature: Institutional Leverage

When navigating bureaucracy or social labyrinths, roll a d20 as your Hope die. On success (10+), gain 1 Favor Token. Spend Tokens to auto-pass permits or expose institutional flaws.

Frontier Colony

Grit, Rust, and Shared Hardship

Neighbors who'd share their last water ration. Practical ingenuity is your creed.

Feature: Scrap-Savvy Ingenuity

Improvise tools from junk: Roll your Fear die (e.g., d6/d8) for effect. On 1-3, it breaks spectacularly. Once per rest, reroll if you sacrifice a personal item.

Void Station

Pressurized Cans in the Dark

A pressurized can hurtling through nothing. Claustrophobic? Maybe. But you breathed recycled air with a dozen species.

Feature: Cross-Species Intuition

When negotiating with aliens or adapting tech, you have 3 Linguistic Tokens. Spend 1 to reroll Hope OR treat Fear as Hope for negotiations. Regain all by mediating a faction dispute or 1 after long rest.

Trade Hub

The Chaotic Symphony of Commerce

The chaotic symphony of the bazaar. Smells of spice, ozone, and desperation. Your market intuition sniffs profit (or disaster) a parsec away.

Feature: Market Oracle

When negotiating prices, gamble resources on rumors: Roll Comprehend (DC12). On success, you gain true intel +1 Hope. Critical: Reveal a secret granting 24h Advantage on related rolls.

Research Outpost

Labs Humming Over Silent Ruins

The pursuit of knowing. Scientific method is your scripture — hypothesis, test, analyze.

Feature: Scientific Resonance

Your lab's instruments can detect echoes of ancient technologies. Once per session, invoke Resonance to attune to a creature or artifact: roll Insight or Investigation against DC 14. On success, learn one factual secret and gain advantage on research or social rolls involving that subject.

Free Fleet

Home is the Next Jump

Stellar navigation is in your bones — you navigate pulsar fields like others walk city streets.

Feature: Voidborn Kinship

When recalling stellar hazards or aiding fleet allies, reroll a failed Hope piloting check OR let an ally in your system use your Hope die result as their own.

AgriSynth

Children of the Harvest-Mind

Born beneath the soft, pulsing glow of nutrient lamps, your first breath drawn from air scrubbed by great AgriSynth processors.

Feature: Nourished by Nature

You have advantage on Knowledge checks to stabilize creatures near death and on Survival checks to find or purify food and water in technologically-supported environments.

Axiom

The Chosen of the Unbroken Chain

Your life is a series of rituals, prayers, and proclamations. You are a member of Axiom, bound by unassailable dogma.

Feature: Scriptural Knowledge

You have advantage on checks related to the history, lore, or internal workings of your faith, and on Insight checks to discern falsehoods in conversations about matters of faith or doctrine.

Circuit

The Integrated Self

You chose to become more. You are Circuit, a person who has willingly merged with cybernetics, augmenting mind and body with technology.

Feature: Hardware Proficiency

You have advantage on Computers checks to hack, interface with, or reprogram any device with a digital component, as well as on Thievery checks involving electronic locks or disabling security systems.

Voidfarer

The Edge of the Abyss

Your home is the void. Born on a drifting asteroid, derelict station, or frozen moon at the very edge of known space.

Feature: Cold-Blooded

You have advantage on saving throws to resist damage from extreme cold or vacuum exposure, as well as on Stealth checks made to hide in shadows or behind cover in zero-gravity or low-light environments.

Step 3: Select Your Class and Subclass

Your class is your profession, your vocation, the path you've chosen in the vast darkness of the galaxy. It determines your main abilities, combat capabilities, special powers, and general function within a group of adventurers.

Soldier

The hardened combatant, master of weapons and tactics

Primary Role: Damage, Defense

Domains: Kinetic, Synthesis

Starting Evasion: 12

Starting Hit Points: 8

Hope Feature: Combat Focus (3 Hope) - Gain advantage on all attack rolls until the end of your next turn.

Class Feature: Tactical Expertise - Once per combat, reposition an ally or enemy within Close range.

Subclass Options:

Engineer

The technical genius, capable of building, repairing, and hacking anything

Primary Role: Support, Control

Domains: Tech, Synthesis

Starting Evasion: 11

Starting Hit Points: 6

Hope Feature: Technical Miracle (3 Hope) - Instantly repair a broken device or create a simple tool from available materials.

Class Feature: Jury Rig - Once per rest, improvise a device that grants advantage on a specific type of check for 10 minutes.

Subclass Options:

Pilot

The navigation ace, one with their ship, seeing paths where others see chaos

Primary Role: Mobility, Control

Domains: Void, Tech

Starting Evasion: 11

Starting Hit Points: 6

Hope Feature: Veil Surge (3 Hope) - Gain advantage on piloting tests and ignore one Severe Damage until your next rest.

Class Feature: Stellar Instinct - Advantage on piloting/navigation tests. Once per session, reroll one failed Void-related test; if you spend Hope on navigation, add +1d8.

Subclass Options:

Scoundrel

The ghost in the shadows, expert in stealth, subterfuge, and lethal precision

Primary Role: Damage, Stealth

Domains: Social, Void

Starting Evasion: 12

Starting Hit Points: 5

Hope Feature: Perfect Strike (3 Hope) - Deal maximum damage with an attack and ignore target's armor.

Class Feature: Shadow Step - Once per rest, become invisible for one round or until you attack.

Subclass Options:

Mystic

The Veil's channel, bending reality with the power of their mind

Primary Role: Psionic Power, Support

Domains: Neural, Social

Starting Evasion: 10

Starting Hit Points: 5

Hope Feature: Veil Surge (3 Hope) - Double the effect of your next psionic power.

Class Feature: Mind Touch - Communicate telepathically with a willing creature within Close range.

Subclass Options:

Scholar

The guardian of knowledge, unraveling the secrets of the past

Primary Role: Investigation, Support

Domains: Tech, Social

Starting Evasion: 10

Starting Hit Points: 5

Hope Feature: Eureka Moment (3 Hope) - Instantly recall a crucial piece of information or solve a complex puzzle.

Class Feature: Analytical Mind - Once per session, ask the GM one specific question about a situation and receive a truthful answer.

Subclass Options:

Diplomat

The peace weaver, master of words and social networks

Primary Role: Negotiation, Support

Domains: Social, Synthesis

Starting Evasion: 10

Starting Hit Points: 5

Hope Feature: Perfect Words (3 Hope) - Automatically succeed on a social interaction check.

Class Feature: Network of Contacts - Once per session, call upon a contact for information or a minor favor.

Step 4: Assign Your Traits

Traits are the six fundamental statistics that define your character's innate and trained capabilities. These numerical values are what you'll use to perform most actions in the game.

You have a set of six modifiers to assign to your six traits: +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1. Each modifier must be assigned to a different trait.

Step 5: Create Your Experience

If traits are the "what" of your character's abilities, experiences are the "why." They're the defining moments of your past that have shaped you.

Define a key moment in your character's life. This experience is linked to one of your six traits and provides narrative context for your ability in that area.

Step 7: Set Wealth and Reputation Levels

In the fractured economy of the post-Silence galaxy, wealth is more than just credits in your account; it's your leverage, your safety net, and your ticket to the next opportunity.

Step 8: Select Your Equipment

For initial equipment you can select:

  • 2 tier points for weapons (e.g., 2 tier 1 cost weapons or 1 tier 2 cost weapon)
  • 2 tier points for armor and equipment (same system applies)
